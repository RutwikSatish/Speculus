The Problem
Most supply chain teams find out about disruptions after they happen.
The global supply chain has been rocked by COVID-19, the Russia-Ukraine war, Red Sea shipping disruptions, and escalating US-China trade tensions — all within five years. Every one of these crises was visible in advance to anyone watching the right signals.
The problem is not that the data does not exist. It does. The problem is that turning that data into a structured, ISO-compliant risk register — and keeping it current — requires hours of manual work every week that most supply chain teams simply do not have.

My Approach

Built on frameworks. Grounded in evidence. Transparent about limitations.

Most risk dashboards are built by engineers who read the marketing materials of existing tools. SPECULUS is built by someone who read the primary sources — the ISO standard, the COSO framework, the ASCM SCOR model, and the peer-reviewed academic literature on supply chain risk taxonomy.

The approach is semi-quantitative risk assessment, which ISO 31000:2018 §6.4.3 explicitly endorses as valid when historical probability data is unavailable — which is the case for most supply chain disruption scenarios. Each supplier is scored across five dimensions derived from two primary sources: country governance baselines from World Bank Worldwide Governance Indicators 2024, and live disruption signals from the GDELT 2.0 geopolitical events database.

Every score in the system has a citable source. Every claim in the documentation has a reference. Anything that is estimated or constructed is clearly labeled as such. This is not how most portfolio projects are built. It is how practitioners operate.

Academic and Industry Foundations

Five frameworks. Each applied, not just cited.

Framework How SPECULUS applies it
ISO 31000:2018 Core risk assessment loop (identify → analyse → evaluate → treat → monitor). The living register implements the mandatory continuous monitoring requirement of §6.6. COSO response taxonomy is applied at threshold breach per §6.5.
COSO ERM 2017 Four-response taxonomy: Avoid, Reduce, Share, Accept. Applied based on gap between composite score and user-defined appetite threshold. Response actions are drawn directly from COSO Chapter 8 treatment options.
SCOR v13 (ASCM) Each risk dimension is mapped to its primary SCOR performance attribute: geopolitical → Agility, supplier health → Reliability, logistics → Responsiveness, regulatory → Cost. This translates risk scores into supply chain performance language executives understand.
Zsidisin & Ritchie (2009) Four-source risk taxonomy: Supply, Demand, Manufacturing/Process, Environmental. SPECULUS Module 1 covers Supply and Environmental sources. Demand-side signals are noted as a Module 2 extension. This is the most cited SCRM taxonomy in the peer-reviewed literature.
World Bank WGI 2024 Country baseline risk scores derived from five indicators: Political Stability (PV), Rule of Law (RL), Government Effectiveness (GE), Regulatory Quality (RQ), Control of Corruption (CC). Inverted from 0-100 governance scale to 0-100 risk scale. 38 countries covered. Data year: 2024.
What SPECULUS Actually Does

Six steps from supplier list to executive-ready risk register.

  1. Supplier ingestion

    User uploads a list of supplier names and country codes (CSV or manual entry). No ERP integration required. No IT department needed. Setup time: under 2 minutes.

  2. Risk appetite calibration

    User sets threshold sliders for each of the five ISO 31000 risk dimensions. Any supplier scoring above their threshold triggers a COSO ERM response. Thresholds are fully configurable to the organisation's risk tolerance.

  3. Live signal ingestion

    For each supplier-country pair, SPECULUS queries GDELT 2.0 (geopolitical events, disruption news — free, no API key) and the US Federal Register RSS (tariff and trade policy changes — public domain). Both sources update in near real-time.

  4. Semi-quantitative scoring

    Each supplier is scored across five dimensions using WGI 2024 country baselines plus a signal boost calculated from GDELT news frequency and severity. Composite score is a weighted sum. All weights and formulas are shown transparently in the interface.

  5. COSO ERM response engine

    Suppliers breaching any appetite threshold receive a COSO response classification (Avoid / Reduce / Share / Accept), specific recommended actions, a SCOR performance attribute mapping, and — if a Groq API key is provided — an AI-generated executive risk brief.

  6. ISO 31000 compliant export

    The full risk register exports as CSV or JSON in a format that satisfies ISO 31000 §6.7 documentation requirements. Includes review cadence (weekly/monthly/quarterly based on score), audit trail, and COSO action log.

What Is Verified vs. Estimated

Transparency about the methodology is non-negotiable.

Most tools hide their scoring methodology behind proprietary claims. SPECULUS publishes every formula, every source, and every limitation openly. Here is the honest breakdown:

◈ Verified

ISO 31000, COSO ERM, SCOR frameworks

Applied directly from primary sources. Every framework reference is citable and cross-checked against published standards.

◈ Verified

Country baseline scores

Derived directly from World Bank WGI 2024 dataset. Downloaded, parsed, and inverted programmatically. 38 countries, 5 indicators, data year 2024.

◈ Verified

Live signal data

GDELT 2.0 Doc API (real-time, free, no key required). US Federal Register RSS (public domain). Both are live data sources, not synthetic.

◈ Documented heuristic

Dimension weights

Geopolitical 25%, Supplier Health 25%, Logistics 20%, Single Source 15%, Regulatory 15%. Grounded in practitioner literature. Not from a single paper. Calibrate to your industry.

◈ Documented heuristic

Signal severity scoring

Keyword-based heuristic (critical terms add 3 points, moderate terms add 2). Original algorithm documented in source code. Not from a published paper.

◈ Demo only

Demo mode signals

Pre-seeded signals for the demo portfolio are clearly labeled "DEMO — simulated" in the interface. WGI country scores in demo mode are real. Signals are not.
